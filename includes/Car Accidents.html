Types of Car Accidents

Car accidents can take many different forms, but some of the most common types include:

Rear-end collisions: These occur when one vehicle crashes into the back of another vehicle. Rear-end collisions are often caused by following too closely or distracted driving.

Head-on collisions: These occur when two vehicles collide head-to-head. Head-on collisions are often very serious or fatal.

Side-impact collisions: These occur when the side of one vehicle collides with the side or front of another vehicle. Side-impact collisions can also be serious, especially if the impact occurs on the driver's side.

T-bone collisions: These occur when one vehicle crashes into the side of another vehicle, forming a T-shape. T-bone collisions can be caused by running a red light or stop sign.

Single-vehicle accidents: These occur when a vehicle crashes into a fixed object, such as a tree, telephone pole, or guardrail. Single-vehicle accidents can also be caused by losing control of the vehicle.
